## What This Skill Does

Helps NINE explain AI tools and workflows to clients in plain language. Covers the 4D Framework for AI adoption, ethical considerations, and ready-to-use client education scripts.

## The 4D Framework

A four-stage model for how businesses adopt AI:

| Stage | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| D1 | **Discover** | Understanding what AI can do for their specific business |
| D2 | **Delegate** | Handing off repetitive, defined tasks to AI |
| D3 | **Design** | Building workflows where AI and humans collaborate |
| D4 | **Drive** | AI embedded in the business model — competitive advantage |

Most SMB clients NINE works with are at D1–D2. The pitch is: "we'll help you get to D2 without the technical overhead."

## Explaining AI content to clients

**For restaurant clients:**
> "Instead of hiring a videographer for every dish launch, we use AI to generate cinematic food reels from reference images in hours. You get consistent, high-quality content at a fraction of the cost."

**For health/wellness clients:**
> "AI lets us produce transformation content and expert explainers at scale — the kind of content that builds trust and drives bookings, without a full production crew every time."

**For FMCG clients:**
> "We use AI-generated product visuals and lifestyle reels to test creative angles quickly. You find out what converts before you spend on a full shoot."

## Ethical AI talking points

- AI generates drafts; NINE's creatives review and refine everything
- Client brand voice and visual identity are always human-set
- No fake testimonials, no misleading before/afters
- All AI-generated content disclosed where platform rules require it
- Data privacy: client briefs and assets are not used to train models

## Client objection handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "AI content looks fake" | "The tools have improved dramatically — here's an example we made for a restaurant last month. Most people can't tell." |
| "Will it replace your team?" | "No — AI handles the volume, our team handles the craft. You get both." |
| "I've tried AI and it didn't work" | "Most AI tools need a strong creative brief and brand guide to produce good work. That's what we provide." |
| "Is it ethical?" | "We're transparent about AI use, we always review output, and we never create misleading content." |

## Client education one-pager structure

1. What AI content is (one paragraph, plain language)
2. What NINE does with it (process: brief → generate → review → deliver)
3. What the client gets (speed, volume, consistency)
4. What stays human (strategy, brand voice, quality control)
5. One example relevant to their niche

## Response style

Adapt language to the client's sophistication. Restaurant owner = plain English. CMO at a FMCG brand = can handle more nuance. Always anchor to their specific business context.
